HUES hosts seasonal social events that aim to give
LGBTQ people a place where they can feel safe, have
fun, and meet others with shared identities.

The Come Out

The Come Out, held at The Avenue Café, was a series of monologues where members of the LGBTQ community in the East Lansing/Lansing area told their coming out stories and talked about their experiences as queer people. Dr. Tim Retzloff, a professor at Michigan State University, also read off names of those in the LGBTQ community who have passed away over the years, holding a vigil in remembrance of their lives. Lansing Association for Human Rights and MSU’s The Alliance of Queer and Ally Students also helped host the event. We were ecstatic about the success of this event, as we packed The Avenue with around 200 guests and attracted the attention of local politicians and news sources.

Michigan Pride 2015

HUES had a table at Lansing’s Michigan Pride event, where we distributed educational information about current LGBTQ issues in the Lansing area and promoted the organization to the young adults in the area. We loved meeting and talking with everybody at Michigan Pride, and were excited to see the enthusiasm that was held for the LGBTQ community in Michigan.

Summer Tie Dye

HUES’ first event, The Summer Tie Dye, was held at Patriarche Park, where we gathered for an afternoon of tie-dye, food, and good company. Even though we were rained on for most of the event (as is what seems to always happen to HUES events), we were so excited to see people make it out to spend some time dyeing shirts, socks, and even shoes with us.
